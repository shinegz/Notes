# History of artificial intelligence

**Wikipedia Contributors**

## Abstract

A comprehensive historical overview of AI from antiquity (myths of artificial beings) through the founding of AI research at the 1956 Dartmouth workshop, the AI winters of the 1970s and 1990s, the expert systems boom of the 1980s, the rise of machine learning in the 2000s, to the deep learning and transformer breakthroughs that led to generative AI.

## Key Milestones

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1956 | Dartmouth Workshop — field officially founded; Logic Theorist presented |
| 1974 | Lighthill Report + U.S. Congress pressure → funding cuts (first AI winter) |
| 1980s | Expert systems boom → billion-dollar industry |
| 1990s | AI Winter II — investor enthusiasm wanes |
| Early 2000s | Machine learning applied broadly; powered by hardware + big data |
| 2012+ | Deep learning breakthrough eclipses other methods |
| 2017 | Transformer architecture introduced |
| 2020s | Generative AI applications proliferate |

## Key Concepts

- **Dartmouth Workshop (1956)**: Allen Newell, Herbert Simon present Logic Theorist; attendees become AI research leaders for decades
- **AI Winters**: Periods of reduced funding and interest (1974, 1990s) caused by overpromising and underestimating complexity
- **Expert Systems**: Rule-based systems that revitalized AI investment in the 1980s
- **Machine Learning Renaissance**: Hardware advances + large datasets + mathematical rigor enabled broad ML adoption
- **Deep Learning Era**: Neural networks with many layers surpassed traditional methods across domains
