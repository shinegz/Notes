# AI Index | Stanford HAI

**Stanford Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence (HAI)**

## Abstract

The AI Index is an annual comprehensive report tracking AI's technical progress, economic influence, and societal impact. Published by Stanford HAI, it provides unbiased, rigorously vetted, globally sourced data for policymakers, researchers, journalists, and the general public. The 2026 report highlights a widening gap between AI capabilities and governance preparedness, with data transparency declining across the field.

## Key Contributions

- **Comprehensive annual tracking** of AI technical capabilities, investment, and adoption
- **Globally sourced, rigorously vetted data** recognized by media, governments, and industry
- **Policy-relevant insights** into AI governance gaps and societal impact
- **Independent measurement** addressing declining data transparency in AI research

## Report Focus Areas

| Area | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Technical Progress** | Model capabilities, benchmark performance, research output |
| **Economic Influence** | Investment trends, market adoption, industry deployment |
| **Societal Impact** | Employment effects, accessibility, ethical considerations |
| **Governance Gap** | Frameworks falling behind rapid technical advancement |
| **Data Transparency** | Independent measurement as field transparency declines |

## Key Finding (2026 Report)

> "A widening gap between what AI can do and how prepared we are to manage it."

While AI continues rapid integration into the global economy — with technical capabilities improving, investment accelerating, and adoption spreading — the frameworks needed to govern, evaluate, and understand this technology are falling behind.
